# Convention Review — idiomatic Triton/CUDA vs "what works"

> Agent-swarm audit of every course artifact (26 lecture notebooks + 13 Triton
> exercises + 6 CUDA exercises = 45). **Lens:** does the course teach the *idiom*
> the Triton/CUDA community actually writes, or merely a pattern that produces the
> right answer? **Method:** one reviewer per artifact, then a high-effort
> **adversarial verifier** that defaults to *refuting* each finding — it confirms
> one only when the convention claim is genuinely the community standard, the
> pattern is presented *as canonical*, and it is **not** sign-posted as a
> deliberate stage-appropriate simplification.

**Tally:** 63 findings raised · **14 confirmed** · 49 refuted · 8 were sign-posted simplifications · 9/45 artifacts fully idiomatic.

`CONFIRMED` = the verifier agreed it's a real convention gap worth fixing.
`KEPT` = verifier returned no matching verdict; left for a human look. Refuted
findings are summarized at the bottom for transparency.
